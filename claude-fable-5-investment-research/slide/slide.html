Why the window exists — the mispricings

4–6×farmland gap to Bulgaria/Romania/Hungary (€8.7k/ha vs ~$1.5–2.5k) — and only Ukrainian citizens/entities may buy until a referendum lifts the ban
3–4×EBITDA for profitable Ukrainian IT outsourcers vs 5.9–10× global peers; Diia City locks a 9%/5% tax regime
2–4 yrspayback on gas CHP against a grid that lost 43.5 GW; Ukrenergo pays 5-yr EUR-fixed capacity fees for flexibility
$35Bdefense output capacity (35× since 2022) vs ~⅓ funded domestically — exports legalized 1 Jul 2026 (20% levy), UA names at 2.5–6× revenue vs 10–14× for EU peers
$588B10-yr reconstruction needs (World Bank RDNA5, Feb-2026) — 2.7× GDP of forward demand

What breaks it — and the built-in answer

  • WarEscalation hits land/CHP/UAH → 15% defense hedge + 1%-cost state war insurance; portfolio floor 1.43× nominal.
  • FXUAH step-devaluation (−25% in 2022) → ~62% of portfolio cash flow is USD/EUR-linked (IT exports, EUR-fixed BESS, land as real asset).
  • PeopleMobilization drains the IT firm → Diia City + critical-status booking (50–100% quotas); staff-mix diligence pre-close.
  • StateGovernance/raider/windfall-tax risk (Ferrexpo seizure, 50% bank CIT) → no regulated monopolies, clean-title diligence, regime diversification.
  • ExitThin M&A market if war persists → OVDP liquidity sleeve + assets with standalone cash yield; land trades 340k ha/yr even now.

Method — in one breath

  • Official Derzhstat SDMX data (GVA by sector to 2025, trade, capex, CPI to Jun-2026) → sector screen (11 sectors, 9 weighted criteria).
