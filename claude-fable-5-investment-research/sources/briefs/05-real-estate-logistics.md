### 1.2 Stock destroyed in 2022
- Kyiv region: warehouse/logistics complexes of **~382,000 sqm total area destroyed** by June 2022; **~340,000 sqm net warehouse space wholly (213k) or partially (169k) destroyed = 22% of Kyiv-region stock**; stock fell **1.6 → 1.3 mn sqm** in H1 2022. Clusters along M-06 (Stoyanka, Makariv) and M-07 (Bucha, Hostomel). [Property Forum](https://www.property-forum.eu/news/hows-ukraines-warehouse-market-functioning-during-the-war/12743) **[H]** (note internal figures 213+169=382 gross; "340k net" as quoted).
- National 2022–23: **~450,000 sqm destroyed**; Jan–mid-Apr 2025: another **25,000 sqm** damaged/destroyed (drone/missile strikes ongoing — Kyiv warehouses repeatedly hit in 2025). LIGA.net/NAI Ukraine via [InVenture (UA)](https://inventure.com.ua/uk/analytics/articles/skladska-neruhomist-vidnovlyuye-poziciyi:-komu-vigidno-investuvati-u-2025-roci) **[M]**.

### 1.3 Yields / cap rates **[M — biggest data gap; brokers stopped publishing formal prime yields]**
- Pre-war 2021 benchmark: **~12% cap rate (~8.3-yr payback) across major cities, all CRE classes** — IRE (Ukraine) LLC via [reNews, 15 Jun 2025](https://renews.com.ua/ekonomika/komerciina-neryhomist-iaka-sityaciia-na-rinky-v-ykrayini-ta-sho-privertaye-yvagy-investoriv/).
- 2025 wartime cap rates (IRE/reNews, same source; table is for retail but indicative of CRE repricing): Kyiv **15.5%** (6.4-yr payback), Odesa 15.2%, Dnipro & Lviv **16.6%**, Kharkiv 17.5%; Lviv office 15%.
- Warehouse investor returns 2025: **Kyiv/Lviv Class A 8–10% p.a. net; central regions 6–8%** (CBRE Ukraine via LIGA/[InVenture UA](https://inventure.com.ua/uk/analytics/articles/skladska-neruhomist-vidnovlyuye-poziciyi:-komu-vigidno-investuvati-u-2025-roci)); Alterra Group cites **10–12%** on own projects; aggregator view **10–15% USD, 8–9-yr payback** ([Metkas, 2026](https://metkas.ua/investicii-v-skladsku-neruhomist-2026/)) **[L–M]**. Model suggestion: use **12–14% exit cap for stabilized Kyiv prime logistics**, 15%+ for regions.
- Investment volume: total Ukraine CRE **$300M (2024) → $250M (2025)**, of which warehouse **$31M in 2025 (−50–55% y/y)** — deals postponed on strike risk. Expandia via [InVenture](https://inventure.com.ua/en/news/world/investment-in-ukraines-commercial-real-estate-declined-to-dollar250-million-in-2025) **[H]**.

### 1.4 Development cost vs stabilized value (Kyiv Class A big-box)
- Construction cost: **$550–600/sqm** Class A dry (CBRE via [Property Forum, Apr 2024](https://www.property-forum.eu/news/whats-next-for-ukraines-warehouse-market/18329); was $400–450 two years prior, +30%); alternative estimate **$600–800/sqm Class A, $400–550 B, $250–350 C** with +15–25% y/y cost inflation ([InVenture UA](https://inventure.com.ua/uk/analytics/articles/skladska-neruhomist-vidnovlyuye-poziciyi:-komu-vigidno-investuvati-u-2025-roci)) **[M]**. 2024–25 cost of Class A sqm +7.5% ([open4business/Experts Club](https://open4business.com.ua/en/ukraines-warehouse-real-estate-market-will-shift-from-recovery-to-shortage-of-high-quality-supply-in-2026-experts-club/)).
- Stabilized value: prime rent $5.3/mo = $63.6/sqm/yr net; at 12–13% cap → **~$490–530/sqm** (derived). Sale comps: older stock western regions **$280–300/sqm** (Dec 2024–Apr 2025, [Uvecon GIS](https://gisuvecon.com/main/82/stan-rinku-skladskoyi-neruhomosti-ukrayini-gruden-2024-kviten-2025/?lang=en)); pre-war Kyiv Class A equivalent ~$600–700/sqm. **Implication: value ≈ or below replacement cost ($650–900 all-in incl. land/utilities) → speculative big-box development is marginal without cheap land or pre-lets; explains 2026 pipeline collapse to 90k sqm.** [Derived]
- War insurance largely unavailable/unaffordable for private developers; MIGA guarantees used by institutional players (M10) ([Kosse, Medium, Jul 2025](https://medium.com/@iryna.kosse/ukraines-warehouse-market-is-returning-to-economic-logic-1041ae66e906); [Dragon Capital](https://dragon-capital.com/media/press-releases/norwegian-ukraine-investment-fund-announces-first-investment-initiative-focused-on-m10-lviv-industrial-park/)).

### 1.5 Lviv / western Ukraine

- Industrial RE commissioning shares (Jan–Sep 2025 vs 2021): Lviv **19% vs 16.5%**, Zakarpattia **7.1% vs 2.5%**, Kyiv 32.3% vs 23.5%, Dnipropetrovsk 1.5% vs 8%. National industrial commissioning: 987k sqm 2024 (+16%), 852.5k Jan–Sep 2025 (+23%); pre-construction pipeline 1.18mn sqm (−29% y/y). [GMK Center](https://gmk.center/en/posts/the-volume-of-new-industrial-real-estate-coming-online-in-ukraine-is-growing-by-20-annually/) **[H]**.
- Flagship projects: **M10 Lviv Industrial Park** (Dragon Capital; 23.5 ha, Phase I 14,400 sqm commissioned Feb 2024, Phase II 22,000 sqm; EBRD 35% + Norfund/Ukraine Investment Fund 30%; 10-yr MIGA war-risk guarantee) — [Dragon Capital](https://dragon-capital.com/media/press-releases/dragon-capital-launches-construction-of-phase-ii-of-m10-lviv-industrial-park/), [EBRD](https://www.ebrd.com/home/news-and-events/news/2023/ebrd-invests-in-developing-lviv-industrial-park-in-western-ukraine.html); **Sparrow Park Lviv** (113,000 sqm planned; hit by Russian strike Oct 2025 — western Ukraine is NOT strike-free) — [GEIPP](https://geipp-ukraine.org/en/industrial_parks/sparrow-park-l-viv-industrial-park/), [dev.ua](https://dev.ua/en/news/poblyzu-lvova-horyt-industrialnyi-park-1759646911); **PORT Logistics Center 48,000 sqm** fully commissioned (Alterra) — [open4business](https://open4business.com.ua/en/ukraines-warehouse-real-estate-market-will-shift-from-recovery-to-shortage-of-high-quality-supply-in-2026-experts-club/); Kingspan €280M campus (Lviv).

## 6. $1–5M private-investor economics

**A. Build-to-lease warehouse (yield-on-cost):**
- Big-box Kyiv Class A: all-in $650–900/sqm (constr. $550–800 + land/utilities); NOI ~$60–62/sqm/yr (prime $5.3/mo, 97% occ.) → **YoC ~7–9.5%** [derived from §1.4] — thin vs 12–14% market cap rates → **negative development spread for spec big-box; viable only with pre-let + cheap land or donor-backed risk cover (MIGA-style)**.
- Small-format/light-industrial (250–450 sqm units, LSTK frame): constr. from $250–320/sqm; example 1,000 sqm = $370k total ($320k build + $50k land/utilities), net income $54k/yr → **14.6% net YoC, 6.8-yr payback; multi-unit formats claimed 18–25%** ([Readycon](https://readycon.com.ua/publications/sklad-yak-investytsiia.html) — **contractor marketing, [L], haircut heavily**; rents $5–8/sqm/mo for small blocks are plausible given shortage). Entry tickets: $250–450k premium units; from $40k urban logistics ([Metkas](https://metkas.ua/investicii-v-skladsku-neruhomist-2026/) [L]).
- Lviv variant: rents $6.0–6.5 (+15–20% vs Kyiv), near-zero vacancy, same build cost → YoC ~1–1.5pp higher, but land scarcer/dearer near Lviv ring; Oct 2025 Sparrow Park strike shows residual war risk.

**B. Residential development (Lviv/Kyiv):**
- Hard cost ~$600/sqm (official Mindev index) + land/soft/finance ≈ $800–1,000 all-in [derived, M]; sell-side: Lviv $1,380, Kyiv $1,300–2,011 → **indicative gross development margin ~25–40%**, sustained by eOselia demand (1/3 of new-build sales) and +10–15% expected 2026 price growth; risks: labor shortage, cost inflation +14% y/y, sales pace (transactions still −37% vs 2021).

**C. Land banking (western cities):**
- Agricultural land: Zakarpattia UAH ~190k/ha (national leader), Ivano-Frankivsk 126.6k, Lviv 111.5–118.3k UAH/ha; **+60% since market opening (Jul 2021)** ([Opendatabot](https://opendatabot.ua/en/analytics/land-2025), [InVenture](https://inventure.com.ua/en/news/ukraine/ukraines-farmland-market-in-2025:-what-is-happening-to-agricultural-land-prices)) **[M]**.
- Residential plots near Lviv/Zakarpattia cities: **$2,000–2,400 per sotka (100 sqm)** ([Visit Ukraine](https://visitukraine.today/blog/4489/land-prices-in-ukraine-how-much-does-1-hectare-for-construction-cost-and-what-do-you-need-to-buy)) **[L]**.
- Demand driver: **>19,000 companies relocated registrations 2024–25**; investor demand focused on 1+ ha (manufacturing) and 5–10+ ha (logistics) serviced plots ([Expandia](https://expandia-ukraine.com/en/investment-in-commercial-real-estate-in-ukraine-2025-trends-and-investor-outlook/)) — serviced industrial land near Lviv/Mukachevo is the scarcity asset; foreigners still barred from farmland.

## Explicit gaps / caveats for the model
1. **No formal published prime-yield series** for Ukraine 2022–26 (CBRE/C&W suspended); cap rates above are triangulated from one appraiser (IRE), broker commentary, and payback quotes — treat ±200bp.
2. NBU FSR PDFs (Jun & Dec 2025) blocked to fetcher (HTTP 403) — NBU figures come via secondary coverage; the NBU residential price index itself was not retrieved.
3. Kyiv primary price discrepancy ($1,300–1,400 LUN median vs $2,011 Interfax study avg) is methodological (median asking of starting prices vs class-weighted average) — pick one basis and stay consistent.
4. Western-city % growth since Mar 2021 (Lviv +70%, Uzhhorod +50.7%) — source doesn't state currency; LUN dollar series suggests USD, unconfirmed.
5. No transaction-level cap-rate evidence for warehouse deals (only $31M traded in 2025 — market too thin for reliable comps).
6. Uzhhorod secondary prices, Lviv vacancy % (only "near-zero" qualitative), and 2022 warehouse rent trough are low-confidence/absent.
7. IRE/reNews warehouse rents ($7–8 Kyiv) conflict with CBRE net prime ($5.3) — likely gross incl. VAT/OPEX or small-format city stock; use CBRE net series for institutional modeling.
