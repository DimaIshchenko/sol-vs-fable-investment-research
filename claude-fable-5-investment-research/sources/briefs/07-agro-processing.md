## 2. Value-added trend

- Processed/value-added products (ready foods, flour, dairy, starch): **$4.4bn in 2025 ≈ 20% of agri exports** — [USM](https://en.usm.media/agricultural-products-provided-56-of-ukraines-exports-in-2025-despite-a-decrease-in-the-share/) [HIGH]. Raw grain+oilseeds still ~55–60% of agri value.
- **10% export duty on soybeans and rapeseed effective 4-Sep-2025** (law 13157; falls 1 pp/yr from 2030 to 5%; own-produce farmer exports exempt — in practice the exemption procedure caused export suspensions in Sep-2025). Sources: [APK-Inform](https://www.apk-inform.com/en/news/1550149), [OFI Magazine](https://www.ofimagazine.com/news/ukraines-export-duty-on-rapeseed-and-soyabeans-comes-into-force), [Sayenko Kharenko](https://sk.ua/introduction-of-export-duties-on-soybeans-and-rapeseed-in-ukraine/) [HIGH]. Rationale: crush plants were ~35% underutilized; full loading est. +UAH 7.3bn (~$177M) budget revenue ([MillingMEA](https://millingmea.com/ukraine-imposes-10-export-duty-on-soybeans-to-boost-domestic-processing/)).
- Duty effect: domestic soy/rapeseed prices fell ~10% ([UkrAgroConsult](https://ukragroconsult.com/en/news/10-export-duty-on-rapeseed-and-soybeans-reduces-domestic-prices-in-ukraine/)); processors loaded capacity — 3-crop crush projected **16 Mt (+3%) in MY25/26** despite record-low sunflower crop ([APK-Inform](https://www.apk-inform.com/en/news/1550896)) [HIGH].
- Sunflower seed export duty (legacy, since 1999, now ~10%) keeps raw sunseed exports near zero (35 kt in MY25/26). [HIGH]
- State incentive: special investment agreements with CabMin (Astarta first signee, 2024, for $80M+ soy protein concentrate plant — state support incl. tax breaks) — [IFC press](https://www.ifc.org/en/pressroom/2025/ifc-eu-and-the-netherlands-back-ukraine-s-astarta-to-boost-jobs-agriculture-and-th). EU/DFI money (IFC, EBRD, EU "Food for Impact") is the main capex funding channel ([Kyiv Independent investor guide](https://kyivindependent.com/investorsguidetoukraine/sectors-seeing-investment/)).

## 3. Oilseed crushing

- **Capacity:** ~23 Mt/yr seed-equivalent at start of MY25/26 ([Fastmarkets](https://www.fastmarkets.com/insights/ukrainian-sunflower-oil-sector-losses-mount-amid-intensified-attacks-underutilized-crush-capacity-limits-production-impact/) [HIGH]).
- **Supply vs capacity:** 2025 crops — sunflower ~10.8 Mt (11-yr low), soy ~5.5 Mt, rapeseed ~3.2 Mt → ~19.5 Mt max feedstock, much of soy/rape still exported raw. **Utilization ~50–60%** in MY24/25 and similar/lower in MY25/26 [MED]. Sunflower crush Sep–May MY25/26: **8.5 Mt, −11% y/y, lowest since 2013/14** ([Latifundist, 22-Jun-2026](https://latifundist.com/en/novosti/70501-pererobka-sonyashniku-v-ukrayini-vpala-do-minimumu-za-12-rokiv) [HIGH]). Ukroliyaprom: 8.41 Mt sunseed + 1.32 Mt rapeseed crushed in first 9 months MY25/26.
- **Crush margins:** no clean public $/t series [GAP]. Best proxies:
  - Kernel oilseed EBITDA/t: FY2024 (Jul23–Jun24) segment EBITDA **$83M on 3.2 Mt** (~$26/t seed, incl. $27M insurance one-off); FY2025 **$148M on 3.5 Mt** = ~$42/t seed = **$105 EBITDA/t of oil sold** ([Kernel FY2025 AR](https://www.kernel.ua/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/FY2025_Kernel_Annual_Report.pdf) [HIGH]). Pre-war benchmark $150–200+/t oil [LOW].
  - MY24/25 and MY25/26: repeatedly reported **negative spot margins**; at seed prices UAH 33,000–34,000/t ($790–810/t) "processing margins simply do not exist"; plants idled or switched to rapeseed ([Razom.media](https://razomua.media/en/news/biznes/zavody-perestaly-pereroblyaty-sonyashnyk-i-tse-vzhe-poznachayetsya-na-eksporti-oliyi), [UkrAgroConsult](https://ukragroconsult.com/en/news/2025-26-season-may-be-unprofitable-for-ukrainian-oilseed-processors/)) [MED]. Fastmarkets (Dec-2025): "deeply negative crush margins" amid farmer seed retention + diesel deficit + drone strikes on plants.
  - Structural read: bargaining power shifted to farmers because capacity (23 Mt) ≫ seed supply (~11 Mt sunflower). Soy/rape crush is the margin bright spot post-duty.
- **Capex per tonne of crush capacity:**
  - Astarta oilseed (soy/rape) plant, Khmelnytskyi: **$76M / 400 kt/yr = $190/t** ([Interfax-Ukraine](https://en.interfax.com.ua/news/investments/1052655.html) [HIGH]); launch postponed beyond 2026 due to war risk ([Latifundist](https://latifundist.com/en/novosti/70112-astarta-perenesla-zapusk-zavodu-z-pererobki-olijnih-na-hmelnichchini-a-v-globinomu--planuyut-v-drugij-polovini-roku)).
  - Astarta soy protein concentrate (SPC, higher value-add): >$80M total, $40M IFC loan + $40M US DFC; SPC line start H2 2026 ([IFC](https://www.ifc.org/en/pressroom/2025/ifc-eu-and-the-netherlands-back-ukraine-s-astarta-to-boost-jobs-agriculture-and-th), [Open4Business](https://open4business.com.ua/en/astarta-received-40-mln-from-us-to-build-soybean-processing-plant/)) [HIGH].
  - Historical anchors: Kernel Starokostiantyniv ~$130M/1 Mt announced 2017 (~$130/t; final cost reported higher [LOW]); Bunge Mykolaiv $180M plant+transshipment (2016). Range for model: **$150–250/t/yr greenfield solvent crush, +30–50% for refining/bottling** [MED — triangulated].

## 4. Elevators / storage

- **Capacity:** 52.67 Mt simultaneous storage end-2025 (+0.5 Mt y/y; +1.1%) — [USM](https://en.usm.media/last-year-ukraines-grain-elevator-capacity-increased-to-52-67-million-tons/) [HIGH]. ~80% of 2025 projects = modernization/expansion, only 20% new-build ([InVenture](https://inventure.com.ua/en/news/ukraine/agricultural-companies-invest-in-grain-elevator-modernization-instead-of-building-new-facilities)).
- **War damage (KSE/World Bank RDNA):** destroyed granaries **11.4 Mt** + damaged **3.3 Mt** capacity (cumulative through ~2024) vs ~57 Mt pre-war — [KSE](https://kse.ua/agricultural-war-damages-review/), [World Bank](https://documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/099062524074642129/pdf/P180198-405a6aea-dcd5-4926-9724-151419507961.pdf) [MED].
- **Capex per tonne:** no clean 2025 Ukraine figure [GAP]. Generic steel-silo benchmarks: ~$80/t large on-site; $100–140/t transportable ([FAO](https://www.fao.org/4/t1838e/T1838E1c.htm), [Prairies Partners](https://www.prairiespartners.com/how-much-does-it-cost-to-build-a-grain-silo/)). Full Ukrainian elevator complex (silos + dryer + intake/rail loading) historically **$150–250/t** [LOW — verify with Elevatorist/KMZ Industries quotes].
- **Tariffs:** corn drying ~**180 UAH/t-% (~$4.3)** at Prometey network, unchanged since MY22/23 ([Prometey/Elevatorist](https://prometey.org.ua/en/post/elevatoristcom-nerivnomirnij-popit-na-zberiganna-zerna-ta-zdesevlenna-susinna-so-vidbuvaetsa-na-rinku-elevatornih-poslug/) [MED]).
- **Profitability signals:** mixed. Demand uneven; 2025 privatization auctions of state elevators drew **zero deals** — legal + logistics risk killed demand ([InVenture](https://inventure.com.ua/en/news/ukraine/zero-deals-in-2025:-why-grain-asset-privatization-failed-in-ukraine) [HIGH]) — i.e., distressed acquisition entry possible below replacement cost. Historical transshipment terminal payback 5–6 yrs on $100M ([ShareUAPotential/Bunge](http://shareuapotential.com/Emitents/bunge.html) [LOW, pre-war]).

## 5. Logistics

- **Corridor:** Ukrainian unilateral sea corridor (since Aug/Sep-2023) cumulative **168.9 Mt cargo incl. ~100 Mt grain** by early 2026; **CY2025: 73.2 Mt cargo, 38.1 Mt grain, 5,789 vessels** ([GMK Center](https://gmk.center/en/news/ukrainian-sea-corridor-ensured-export-of-more-than-25-million-tons-of-cargo-in-2025/), [KMU](https://www.kmu.gov.ua/en/news/minrozvytku-ukrainskym-morskym-korydorom-perevezeno-120-mln-tonn-vantazhiv), [Intent](https://intent.press/en/news/economy/2026/odesa-region-ports-exported-147-million-tons-of-cargo-to-50-countries/)) [MED].
- Six operating ports: Odesa, Chornomorsk, Pivdennyi (POC) + Danube (Reni, Izmail, Ust-Dunaysk). Mykolaiv still closed ([S&P Global](https://www.spglobal.com/energy/en/news-research/latest-news/agriculture/021325-ukrainian-traders-assess-future-grain-trade-flows-amid-prospects-of-wars-end)).
- **Costs vs pre-war:**
  - War-risk premium Dec-2025: **0.45–0.55% of hull value per voyage** (peak 2023: 1.0–1.2%) ([Agroreview](https://agroreview.com/en/newsen/overview-war-risk-premiums-trends/), [UkrAgroConsult](https://ukragroconsult.com/en/news/war-risk-premiums-for-ukrainian-and-russian-black-sea-ports-are-rising/)) [HIGH].
  - POC port elevation cost: **$9/t (Feb-2025)** vs $15/t (Oct-2024) ([S&P Global](https://www.spglobal.com/energy/en/news-research/latest-news/agriculture/021325-ukrainian-traders-assess-future-grain-trade-flows-amid-prospects-of-wars-end)) [HIGH].
  - Danube route (residual): truck central-Ukraine→Reni/Izmail ~$65/t (~2× pre-war); shallow-port transshipment $15–18/t; barge to Constanta ~€26/t; Danube share of sea exports fell **45% (2023) → 13% (2024)** ([USDA AMS Ukraine Grain Transportation, Jun-2025](https://www.ams.usda.gov/sites/default/files/media/UkraineJune2025.pdf)) [HIGH].
  - Rail 2025: grain-wagon lease **UAH 3,000–3,500/day**; working fleet down 32k→24–25k wagons; throughput 2.2–2.6 Mt/mo vs 4 Mt/mo peak need; 4–5 working days/month lost to air alarms ([USM agrologistics](https://en.usm.media/agrologistics-of-ukraine-in-2025-life-in-the-new-normal/)) [HIGH].

| Rapeseed | 452 | 533 |

| Wheat | −101 | 244 |
| Barley | 53 | 121 |

Avg realized prices 2024: corn $190/t, sunflower $553/t, wheat $179/t, rapeseed $480/t, soy $354/t. 2025 EBITDA/ha not yet published [GAP]; directionally strong for oilseeds, weaker for corn. MinAgro said 2024 crop production ~break-even for grains, profitable for oilseeds ([minagro.gov.ua](https://minagro.gov.ua/en/news/in-2024-crop-production-will-break-even-and-the-profitability-of-livestock-production-will-decrease-ministry-of-agrarian-policy-and-food-of-ukraine)) — the agroholding survey skews better than the national average.

**Land values (link to EBITDA):** 2025 avg — legal entities **UAH 74,323/ha (~$1,770)**, individuals UAH 47,246/ha; national avg UAH 55–60k/ha; West: Ivano-Frankivsk UAH 165,615/ha (~$3,950), Ternopil UAH 126,068/ha; frontline Kherson/Mykolaiv/Zaporizhzhia UAH 35–38k/ha (~$850–900). +60% since market opened (Jul-2021). Sources: [Opendatabot](https://opendatabot.ua/en/analytics/land-2025), [KMU](https://www.kmu.gov.ua/en/news/minekonomiky-rynok-zemli-u-2025-rotsi-pereishov-do-stabilnoi-ta-prohnozovanoi-modeli-rozvytku), [Agroreview](https://agroreview.com/en/newsen/crops/agricultural-land-prices-ukraine-reached/) [HIGH]. Implied multiple: ~$1,800/ha price vs $250–450/ha normalized EBITDA → **4–7× EBITDA/ha, or 15–25% unlevered yield** — extremely cheap vs Poland/Romania ($10–15k/ha) [MED — computed].

## 7. Niche high-margin segments + EU regime

- **EU DCFTA revision:** agreement in principle 30-Jun-2025, **in force 29-Oct-2025**, binding ≥3 years to 2028+. Key TRQs (duty-free volumes): wheat **1.3 Mt** (+30 kt flour — new), poultry **90→120 kt (+33%)**, white sugar **20→100 kt (+400%)**, honey **6→35 kt (+483%)**; 31 (was 36) products remain tariff-capped; access above quota conditional on Ukraine aligning with EU production standards by ~2028; new bilateral safeguard. Sources: [EC DG Trade](https://policy.trade.ec.europa.eu/eu-trade-relationships-country-and-region/countries-and-regions/ukraine_en), [Poultry Site](https://www.thepoultrysite.com/news/2025/07/eu-raises-import-quotas-for-ukrainian-poultry-wheat-and-sugar), [Rada](https://www.rada.gov.ua/en/news/News/267658.html), Regs (EU) 2025/1132 & 2025/2199 via [AGRINFO](https://agrinfo.eu/book-of-reports/ukrainian-tariff-rate-quotas/) [HIGH]. Wheat quota **was binding in MY25/26** and cited by UGA as an export drag.
- **Organic:** 2024 exports **243 kt / $141M**; Ukraine = **#3 organic supplier to EU** (203.9 kt = 7.7% of EU organic imports) — [Organicinfo](https://organicinfo.ua/en/news/ua-organic-export-2024/) [HIGH]. Organic berries 2025: 15.4 kt (−12.5% vol), **€39.8M (+8.5% val)**; Ukraine ≈20% of EU organic raspberry imports, ~30% blackberry, near-100% organic blueberry ([Interfax](https://en.interfax.com.ua/news/economic/1175817.html), [FreshPlaza](https://www.freshplaza.com/europe/article/9846591/ukraine-exports-15-400-tons-of-organic-berries-in-2025/)).
- **Honey:** ~50 kt/yr exports; avg price $2,222/t (Mar-2025) → ~**$110M/yr**; new EU quota 35 kt materially de-risks; 2026 volumes may fall 10–20% (weak crop) — [AgroPortal](https://agroportal.ua/en/news/ukraina/eksport-ukrajinskogo-medu-cogo-roku-skorotitsya-na-10-20) [MED].
- **Dairy:** butter exports **more than doubled in 2025**, >50% to EU on record EU prices ([USDA FAS Dairy Annual UP2025-0030](https://apps.fas.usda.gov/newgainapi/api/Report/DownloadReportByFileName?fileName=Dairy+and+Products+Annual_Kyiv_Ukraine_UP2025-0030.pdf)) [MED].
- **Poultry (MHP model):** MHP 2025 — revenue $3.8bn (+24%), **EBITDA $641M**; Ukraine poultry output 926.3 kt (+8%), exports 368.6 kt; EU division (Perutnina + UVESA Spain) output 249.2 kt (+70%), EU-segment revenue $1.01bn (+76%), EBITDA $122M (+37%) — the model is now "produce cheap in Ukraine + acquire EU processing/brands" ([Investing.com Q4-2025](https://www.investing.com/news/company-news/mhp-q4-2025-slides-revenue-surges-44-but-net-loss-signals-margin-pressure-93CH-4660173), [PoultryWorld](https://www.poultryworld.net/the-industrymarkets/market-trends-analysis-the-industrymarkets-2/mhp-scales-down-poultry-production-in-ukraine-expands-in-the-eu/)) [HIGH]. Sector poultry exports $1.15bn/458 kt → avg $2,510/t.

## 8. $2–5M investor entry points

| Play | Capex | Economics | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Berry farm (export, frozen/organic)** | Blueberry ~**$30k/ha**; raspberry/strawberry from **$12k/ha** ([Agroreview](https://agroreview.com/en/newsen/crops/why-blueberry-cultivation-profitable-business/), [EastFruit](https://east-fruit.com/en/news/a-second-raspberry-boom-in-ukraine/)) → $2–5M funds 60–150 ha raspberry incl. cold store, or ~80–120 ha blueberry | Blueberry yield 10–15 t/ha; export $3.5–4/kg; profit from year ~5; raspberry export up to $4.2/kg. Minimum viable export project ≈50 ha + cold storage. Revenue potential $35–50k/ha at maturity (blueberry) | MED |
| **Small/mid elevator (10–30 kt)** | New build ~$150–250/t → $2–5M buys 10–25 kt greenfield [LOW]; **buying distressed/state assets currently below replacement** (2025 privatizations failed at zero bids — negotiating leverage) | Revenue = drying, storage, transshipment; utilization risk high in over-supplied West, better near rail/border; pre-war terminal paybacks 5–6 yrs, wartime longer | LOW-MED |
| **Small crush (soy/rape focus, 20–100 t/day)** | Equipment $65–180k (China) + site/works; realistic turnkey $1–4M [LOW]; economics currently favored by 10% soy/rape export duty (feedstock discount ~10%) | Sunflower crush margins negative at scale — avoid sunflower; soy/rape toll-crushing for feed mills more defensible; SPC/lecithin = the value-add frontier (Astarta) | LOW |
| **Farmland accumulation via UA legal entity** | ~$1,500–2,500/ha central regions, cap 10k ha | Normalized EBITDA $250–450/ha → 15–25% unlevered yield + convergence upside toward EU land values on accession trajectory; frontline discount ~50% | MED |

**Cross-cutting risks:** drone/missile strikes on port and crush infrastructure, 50% regional diesel deficits, rail wagon inflation, wheat TRQ binding until standards alignment (~2028), war-duration variable.
