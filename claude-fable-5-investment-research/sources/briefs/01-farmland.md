## 1. Market rules & timeline (confirmed as of 2026)

| Date | Change |
|---|---|
| 1 Jul 2021 | Market opened to **individuals (Ukrainian citizens only), 100 ha cap** (Law 552-IX). Sales halted Feb–May 2022 (invasion), registries reopened May 2022 |
| 1 Jan 2024 | **Phase 2**: Ukrainian **legal entities** (100% Ukrainian-citizen/state/community ownership, incl. UBOs) may buy; **cap raised to 10,000 ha per buyer — for both individuals and legal entities** (KSE Q4-2024 review, p.14) |
| 2026 status | **Foreigners/foreign-owned entities still banned**; lifting requires a **national referendum — none scheduled**. Exception: banks (incl. foreign-capital banks) may acquire farmland via mortgage enforcement but must resell within 2 years. No cap/eligibility changes found in 2025–H1 2026 |

Sources: [KSE Land Market Review Q4 2024 (PDF)](https://kse.ua/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Land-Market-review-Q4-.pdf); [CMS Law-Now, Jan 2024](https://cms-lawnow.com/en/ealerts/2024/01/ukrainian-market-for-agricultural-land-opens-doors-for-additional-investments); [Bimaris legal guide 2026](https://bimaris.legal/insights/can-foreigners-buy-land-in-ukraine); [president.gov.ua](https://www.president.gov.ua/en/news/prezident-pidpisav-zakon-shodo-obigu-zemel-silskogospodarsko-60901); [Atlantic Council on foreigner ban](https://www.atlanticcouncil.org/blogs/ukrainealert/ban-on-farmland-sales-to-foreigners-risks-starving-ukraine-of-investment/)

## 6. Convergence/forecast analyses

- **KSE/FREE Network (Deininger, Nizalov et al., Mar 2024)**: farmland market cap **$35.5bn** at current prices (+$5.5bn since opening); adding legal entities expected to raise prices **+40%** → market cap **~$50bn**; land-collateral lending potential **$17.5bn → $35bn**; liberalization worth **+1–2.7% GDP/yr over 3 years**. Legal entities pay >44% above NMV floor in half of deals vs +1.5% for individuals. URL: [freepolicybriefs.org](https://freepolicybriefs.org/2024/03/20/farmland-market-liberalization-ukraine/)
- **Market cap actual**: UAH 2.154 trn ≈ **$51.9bn** by end-May 2025 (opendatabot via InVenture) — already at the FREE-Network scenario level.
- **Latifundist (2026)**: expected appreciation **10–15%/yr** (not guaranteed); ProZorro auctions in central Ukraine reaching **$6,000/ha**; 2026 prices rise on peace-talk scenarios, flat if war continues.
- **KSE heterogeneity result**: mid-large producers (2,500–7,000 ha) pay **+21%** vs small; pure financial "investors" pay **–7.9%** — no crowding-out of farmers found (KSE Q4-2024, Table 4).
- **Rabobank (2025)**: UA accession adds 41 mln ha (+25% EU farmland), only +4% EU ag value-added ([ukragroconsult](https://ukragroconsult.com/en/news/ukraines-eu-accession-would-increase-the-blocs-agricultural-value-added-by-only-4-rabobank/)).
- **GAP**: no formal published KSE/World Bank/investment-bank model of UA land-price convergence to EU levels upon accession was found. The convergence case rests on the RO/PL precedent (post-2007 CAP-driven convergence documented in [MDPI Sustainability 16(24):10982](https://www.mdpi.com/2071-1050/16/24/10982)) plus the price-gap table above.

## 7. Practical constraints

**War damage/access**: ~20% of territory (133,300 km²) potentially mine/ERW-contaminated incl. occupied areas (Jan 2025, [Landmine Monitor/Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landmines_in_Ukraine)); ~10% of ag land contaminated, ~5 mln ha unsowable (2023, [Mind.ua](https://mind.ua/en/news/20256206-10-of-agricultural-land-in-ukraine-is-mined)); up to 8 mln ha farmland affected (6 mln in occupied areas) ([CSIS](https://www.csis.org/analysis/demining-ukraines-farmland-progress-adaptation-and-needs)); ~20% of arable lost to occupation (112.ua headline, 2026 — could not fetch, low confidence); demining cost estimate $37.6bn (RDNA); 2024: 232,106 ha of ag land cleared. KSE: 20–25% of land inaccessible; land market share in Luhansk/Donetsk/Zaporizhzhia 0.33–0.60% cumulative vs 2–3.6% in central oblasts.

**Price floor**: until **1 Jan 2030**, sale price of formerly-moratorium (pai) land **cannot be below NMV** (avg arable NMV 28,924 UAH/ha; effectively anchors official prices) — [WEAGRO](https://weagro.ua/en/blog/purchase-and-sale-of-agricultural-lands/), KSE.

**Taxes/transaction costs** ([WEAGRO](https://weagro.ua/en/blog/purchase-and-sale-of-agricultural-lands/)):
- Seller PIT: **0%** first sale/yr if held >3 yrs; **5%** second sale; **18%** third+; military levy applies when PIT does — WEAGRO cites 1.5% but levy was raised to **5% from Dec 2024** ([PwC Tax Summaries](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/ukraine/individual/significant-developments)) — **flag: verify current levy on land sales**.
- Buyer: state duty **1%** of price; notary 3,000–10,000 UAH; registration 1,000–2,000 UAH; valuation 2,000–4,000 UAH. KSE: total transaction cost **12,000–15,000 UAH/deal** — prohibitive on 2-ha plots (can reach 5–20% of plot value).
- Holding: land tax (local, % of NMV) + **minimum tax liability (MTL) floor: 1,400 UAH/ha/yr** for >50%-arable land during martial law (700 UAH otherwise); waived in occupied/combat territories (KSE Q4-2024, p.33). Ag producers: Group IV single tax on NMV.
- **All payments must be cashless; buyer citizenship/UBO verification by notary.**

**Building a 1,000–3,000+ ha portfolio via a Ukrainian LLC**: legally permitted (10,000 ha cap incl. affiliates; UBOs must be Ukrainian citizens — a foreign private investor CANNOT hold beneficial ownership; this is the binding constraint). Mechanically feasible but slow: avg plot 2.24 ha → ~450–1,350 separate deals for 1–3k ha; 2024 evidence: 1,517 entities averaged only 35 ha each; largest single-region legal-entity purchases concentrated in Poltava (21%), Dnipropetrovsk (12%). Alternatives used in practice: acquiring lease/emphyteusis rights (~$500/ha cheaper), buying farms as corporate M&A, state Land Bank leases (66-ha avg lots, 14-yr terms).
