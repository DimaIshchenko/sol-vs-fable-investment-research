## 1. Sovereign eurobonds (post-Aug-2024 restructuring)

**Structure** ([White & Case](https://www.whitecase.com/insight-alert/ukraine-concludes-historic-restructuring-us205-billion-international-bonds), [bne IntelliNews](https://www.intellinews.com/ukraine-avoids-default-with-generous-bond-restructuring-deal-334892/), [NV](https://english.nv.ua/business/ukraine-restructures-debt-37-write-off-and-new-bond-terms-50439549.html)):
- $20.47bn of old bonds exchanged (97.38% acceptance, settled ~Sep 2024); **37% nominal haircut**.
- **Series A** (4 series: Feb 2029 / 2034 / 2035 / 2036; split 12.5%/32.5%/30%/25%): coupon **1.75% from Feb 2025, stepping to 7.75% after 2034**; amortization starts 2029.
- **Series B** (4 series: Feb 2030 / 2034 / 2035 / 2036; split 9.5%/35.5%/30%/25%): coupon **0% until Aug 2027, then 3%, stepping to 7.75%**; B-2035/36 carry a GDP-linked **principal reinstatement of up to 12% of the haircut, collectively capped at $2.916bn**, tested 1 Feb 2030 on 2028 GDP (real GDP ≥ IMF baseline +3% plus nominal condition).
- Outstanding (2036 series): A-2036 **$2.456bn**, B-2036 **$1.356bn**; implied totals ≈ **$9.8bn A + $5.4bn B ≈ $15.2bn** (computed from series splits — *medium confidence*). Source: [aryadeniz Substack deep dive, 19 Dec 2025](https://aryadeniz.substack.com/p/deep-dive-pricing-peace-ukraine-2036).

## 2. GDP warrants → restructured away (Dec 2025)

- Original terms: $3.239bn notional (2015 deal); pay if real GDP growth >3% **and** nominal GDP > $125.4bn: **15% of growth between 3–4% + 40% above 4%** (× value of 1% of GDP); capped at 1% of GDP only for 2021–25, **uncapped after** — [Reuters explainer](https://www.investing.com/news/economy-news/explainerwhat-are-ukraines-gdp-warrants-and-why-are-they-creating-problems-for-kyiv-4002639).
- Saga: first deal rejected (2025); warrants **88/89** after 6 Nov 2025 cleansing statement (holders demanded 140% of par, CRM up to $7.475bn) — [Octus, 13 Nov 2025](https://octus.com/resources/articles/ukraines-official-creditors-expected-to-oppose-warrantholders-c-bond-demands-on-cot-grounds-warrants-rise-to-89-despite-significant-disagreements/).
- **Final deal (vote 99.06%, settled end-Dec 2025):** $2.6bn warrants → **$3.497bn "C" notes due 2032** (≈134.5% of par) + ~$34m B notes; Ukraine cancelled $604m self-held warrants; instrument fully retired. C-note terms: coupon **4% to Feb 2027 → 5.5% to Aug 2029 → 7.25%**; amortization **45% Feb 2030, 45% Feb 2031, 10% Feb 2032**. Warrants traded **>101** on announcement. MinFin: removed $6–20bn of contingent 2025–41 payouts. Sources: [MinFin](https://mof.gov.ua/en/news/ukraine_announces_successful_restructuring_of_gdp-linked_warrants_saving_government_billions_of_dollars_in_post-war_period-5496), [Ukrinform, 18 Dec 2025](https://www.ukrinform.net/rubric-economy/4071203-public-debt-restructuring-ukraine-exchanges-gdplinked-bonds.html), [LIGA](https://finance.liga.net/en/ekonomika/novosti/ukraine-completes-gdp-warrant-restructuring-eliminating-major-fiscal-risk), [Kyiv Post](https://www.kyivpost.com/post/65917). *Coupon start conflicting in one source (4.5%) — 4/5.5/7.25 schedule per LIGA is higher confidence.* **Gap: no C-note secondary price found for mid-2026.**

## 6. Capital controls for resident individuals (July 2026)

- **E-limits (pre-war EUR 200k/yr investment allowance) remain suspended** under NBU Res. 18 (24 Feb 2022). NBU's liberalization strategy: retail/portfolio investment abroad is **Stage 3 — still pending**; 2025–Jan 2026 easing packages were corporate-only (dividends, loans, Defence City). Sources: [Chambers Banking Regulation 2026](https://practiceguides.chambers.com/practice-guides/banking-regulation-2026/ukraine/trends-and-developments), [CMS](https://cms.law/en/ukr/legal-updates/national-bank-of-ukraine-to-continue-liberalisation-of-fx-restrictions-and-introduce-a-nuanced-framework-for-2026), [Lexology](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=266c0763-cac8-405a-ae1c-15648dd1d6b6). *One retail guide claims a €200k/yr investment-transfer cap is usable in 2026 ([finhub](https://finhub.org.ua/yak-popovnyty-interactive-brokers-cherez-wise-sepa-ta-swift-u-2026-roczi/)) — contradicts primary sources; treat as low confidence.*
- **What actually works:** cross-border **card P2P ≤ UAH 100,000/month**; "quasi-cash" (funding brokerage accounts) **banned from UAH accounts**, allowed **from FX card accounts within the UAH 100k/month equivalent**; Wise/SEPA gateways used in practice (~0.5% cost; some gateways closed in Jan 2026); SWIFT for investment purposes generally unavailable to individuals. [Engage](https://engage.org.ua/obmezhennya-nbu-na-perekaz-koshtiv-detalnyj-oglyad-2026/), [Fair.org.ua](https://www.fair.org.ua/limit-na-perekaz-koshtiv-aktualni-pravyla-ta-nyuansy-2026/), [finhub](https://finhub.org.ua/yak-popovnyty-interactive-brokers-z-ukrayiny-vsi-sposoby-komisiyi-ta-nyuansy/).
- **IBKR accepts Ukrainian residents** (individual accounts); the binding constraint is funding, ~UAH 1.2m/yr (~$27k) via card routes. Foreign investment income is taxable (18% + 5% levy), unlike OVDP. [IBKR country list](https://www.interactivebrokers.com/en/accounts/open-account-country-list.php), [KrokFin](https://krokfin.com.ua/en/brokers/interactive-brokers/).
- Practical consequence: **onshore OVDP (tax-free) vs offshore anything (capped, taxed)** — the eurobond/warrant trade is mostly accessible only to Ukrainians with pre-existing offshore accounts/funds. **If the investor's $10M is already offshore, the constraint disappears for offshore instruments — and bringing new FX capital INTO Ukraine (charter capital) creates an "investment limit" that legalizes later repatriation.**
