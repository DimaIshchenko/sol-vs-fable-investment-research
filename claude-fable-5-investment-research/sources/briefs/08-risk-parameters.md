### Depreciation rates for modeling (calc)
- **Long run 2000 → mid-2026:** ~5.43 (end-2000) → 44.43 = 8.2x over 25.5 yrs = **~8.6%/yr average** [MED]. NOT smooth: step devaluations in 2008 (5.05→7.8, ~−35%), 2014–15 (8.0→24+, ~−65%), 2022 (27.3→36.6, −25%), interspersed with multi-year stability.
- **War period end-2021 → mid-2026:** 27.28 → 44.43 = **~11.4%/yr** (calc).
- **Managed-float period Oct 2023 → Jul 2026:** 36.6 → 44.4 = **~7.2%/yr** (calc) — NBU has smoothed depreciation to roughly track the inflation differential.
- **Forecasts:** NBU publishes **no FX forecast** (inflation-targeting). Only official forward anchor: budget-2026 average of 45.6. **GAP: no official UAH path 2027–2030; model via inflation differential (NBU inflation 9.4%/6.5%/5.0% for 2026/27/28 vs USD ~2.5%) ⇒ implied ~3–7%/yr depreciation as neutral baseline.**

## 2. Capital controls — status mid-2026

Regime: extensive wartime controls, eased in packages (largest: May 2025; latest major: Resolutions No. 2/3, effective **14 Jan 2026**). All [HIGH] unless noted.

**Companies (residents) CAN:**
- Pay for imports of goods/services freely (current-account trade open).
- **Repatriate "new" dividends: those accrued for periods from 1 Jan 2023** (cutoff extended by Resolution No. 95, Aug 2025), **cap EUR 1 mn/month** via NBU E-limits; issuer ≥1 yr in operation, investor held stake ≥6 months ([EY](https://www.ey.com/en_ua/technical/ey-ukraine-alert/national-bank-of-ukraine-has-revisited-currency-restrictions), [Chambers](https://chambers.com/articles/ukraine-continuously-eases-currency-transfer-rules), [KPMG](https://kpmg.com/ua/en/home/media/press-releases/2025/08/nbu-relaxes-currency-restrictions.html)).
- Use the **"investment limit"** (= new FX equity brought into charter capital from abroad since 12 May 2025) to make otherwise-restricted transfers incl. dividends above the cap ([bank.gov.ua](https://bank.gov.ua/en/news/all/valyutna-liberalizatsiya-trivaye-nbu-vprovadjuye-naybilshiy-paket-pomyakshennya-valyutnih-obmejen-z-pochatku-povnomasshtabnoyi-viyni)).
- Use the **"loan limit"** (= principal of new cross-border FX loans received after 1 Jan 2026, max 12% p.a., no early repayment) for: repaying pre-20-Jun-2023 "old" loans + interest, dividends above cap, old import settlements, financing foreign branches ([CMS](https://cms.law/en/ukr/legal-updates/national-bank-of-ukraine-to-continue-liberalisation-of-fx-restrictions-and-introduce-a-nuanced-framework-for-2026), [Kinstellar](https://www.kinstellar.com/news-and-insights/detail/4002/further-easing-of-currency-restrictions-in-ukraine-focus-on-new-currency-liberalisation-instruments)).
- Service interest on "old" external loans (allowed since May 2024, condition-bound).

**Companies CANNOT:** repatriate pre-2023 accumulated dividends outside the limits; freely repay old-loan principal outside limits; make outbound FDI/portfolio investment; buy FX to hold without an underlying obligation.

**Individuals CAN:** buy non-cash FX **up to UAH 50,000/month** freely + **up to UAH 200,000/month if locked in a ≥3-month deposit**; pay abroad by card for goods/services without limit; make card **P2P/quasi-cash transfers abroad up to UAH 100,000/month** ([Interfax](https://en.interfax.com.ua/news/economic/931590.html), [Lexology/NBU summaries](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=f2f4847e-7dd1-40e9-8472-7c144af63433), [BDO](https://www.bdo.ua/en-gb/ukraine-recovery-1/information-guides-from-bdo-in-ukraine/investments-in-ukraine/currency-limits-and-restrictions)).
**Individuals CANNOT:** transfer savings abroad at scale, buy foreign securities/real estate.

**Roadmap:** NBU's liberalization strategy is **conditions-based (reserves, FX-market balance, inflation), no calendar dates** ([bank.gov.ua/en/markets/liberalization](https://bank.gov.ua/en/markets/liberalization)). Direction 2024→2026 is steady easing; full liberalization tied to macro stability/IMF program.

### MIGA / DFC — foreign-investor-oriented
- MIGA SURE Trust Fund: **>$326 mn guarantees issued by mid-2025**; MIGA insures **cross-border** investments only ⇒ **not available to purely domestic investors** [HIGH] ([MIGA](https://www.miga.org/migas-ukraine-response)).
- **DFC–MIGA PRI framework signed 25 Jun 2026** for the US-Ukraine Reconstruction Investment Fund (URIF; $150 mn initial capitalization; first investment Mar 2026) ([DFC](https://www.dfc.gov/media/press-releases/dfc-miga-sign-agreement-advance-political-risk-insurance-ukraine), [Interfax](https://en.interfax.com.ua/news/economic/1179569-amp.html)).
- **Modeling takeaway:** domestic investor's insurable war-risk cost ≈ **1% of sum insured effective** (post-subsidy, capped at UAH 3 mn subsidy) vs **3.5–12% unsubsidized**; capacity rationed.

## 5. Expropriation / governance risk

**Base-rate events during martial law (2022–2026):**
- **Nov 2022:** requisition of 5 strategic companies — **Ukrnafta, Ukrtatnafta, Motor Sich, AvtoKrAZ, Zaporizhzhiatransformator** — under the martial-law alienation law; state must return or compensate **within 1 year after martial law ends** ([RFE/RL](https://www.rferl.org/a/ukraine-nationalizes-five-companies-war-effort/32119432.html), [Interfax-Ukraine](https://en.interfax.com.ua/news/economic/870579.html), [Kyiv Independent](https://kyivindependent.com/defense-ministry-uses-martial-law-to-seize-oligarchs-assets/)).
- **Jul 2023:** **Sense Bank** nationalized (sanctioned Fridman/Aven/Kosogov) ([Interfax](https://interfax.com/newsroom/top-stories/91541/)).
- **2025–26:** Ferrexpo's Poltava GOK 49.5% stake seized via ARMA (see liquid-instruments brief).
- Pattern: seizures targeted (a) defense-strategic assets and (b) Russia-linked/sanctioned owners. **No broad expropriation of ordinary domestic/foreign private business recorded** [HIGH]. Residual risks: courts, raider schemes, ARMA asset management, AMCU discretion — per [US State Dept 2025 Investment Climate Statement](https://www.state.gov/reports/2025-investment-climate-statements/ukraine).
- **Treaty protection:** 70+ BITs; US-Ukraine BIT in force since 1996; ICSID + NY Convention member ([Legal500](https://www.legal500.com/developments/thought-leadership/investment-protection-in-ukraine-during-wartime-bits-icsid-and-local-remedies/), [UNCTAD](https://investmentpolicy.unctad.org/international-investment-agreements/countries/219/ukraine)). **BITs protect foreign investors; domestic investors rely on Ukrainian courts — weaker protection** [HIGH].
- **GAP: no quantitative expropriation base rate; observable rate ~6 significant forced transfers in 4.5 war years, all in the two categories above.**

## 6. Mobilization / labor risk

**Bronyuvannia (staff booking) rules 2026** [HIGH]:
- From **1 Feb 2026:** reserved employees must earn ≥ **UAH 21,617/mo** (2.5 min wages); from **1 Jun 2026:** enterprise average wage ≥ **UAH 25,941** (3 min wages) for critical status ([Visit Ukraine](https://visitukraine.today/blog/8618/reservation-of-employees-in-2026-new-rules-for-businesses-part-time-workers-and-critical-enterprises), [inkorr](https://inkorr.com/en/amp/bronuvanna-pracivnikiv-u-2026-roci-novi-limiti-zarplatnij-cenz-ta-termini-321925), [J&L](https://www.j-l.consulting/articles/new-2026-employee-reservation-rules/)).
- Quota: **50% of conscription-age male staff** bookable for ordinary critical enterprises; **100%** for defense-industry; one enterprise per person ([sud.ua](https://sud.ua/en/news/publication/364889-bronyuvannya-2026-uryad-zminiv-kriteriyi-kritichnosti-pidpriyemstv-khto-v-zoni-riziku)).
- Existing criticality decisions **expire 1 Sep 2026** unless re-confirmed by 10 Aug 2026 ([sud.ua](https://sud.ua/en/news/publication/365821-broniuvannia-ponad-limit-biznes-mozhe-vtratyty-status-krytychno-vazhlyvoho)). Diia e-reservation live from Mar 2026.

**Labor shortage severity:**
- **67–75% of enterprises report staff shortages** (Mar 2026 surveys); ~230,000 open vacancies vs 96,000–141,000 registered unemployed ([OSW](https://www.osw.waw.pl/en/publikacje/osw-commentary/2026-05-12/high-unemployment-and-labour-shortages-paradoxes-ukraines), [VoxUkraine](https://voxukraine.org/en/war-european-integration-and-workforce-scarcity-what-changes-does-ukraine-s-labor-market-need-in-2026-key-takeaways-from-a-roundtable), [GMK](https://gmk.center/en/posts/an-unsolved-problem-staff-shortages-have-become-the-main-problem-for-ukrainian-businesses/)).
- Unemployment: **~11.3%** (ILO-modeled) to **15.5%** (Mar 2026 survey) [MED].

**Wages (Derzhstat):** avg **UAH 30,356 (Mar 2026)**, **UAH 30,961 (May 2026)**; Kyiv 47,115; min wage **UAH 8,647** from 1 Jan 2026. Nominal wage growth **~20% y/y through 2025**, expected ~11% in 2026 — **real wage growth above productivity = structural margin pressure** ([Ukranews](https://ukranews.com/en/news/1161030-in-may-the-average-salary-for-ukrainians-rose-to-nearly-31-000-hryvnias-according-to-the-state), [Mezha](https://mezha.net/eng/bukvy/cc2b8f70_ukraine-s_average_pay/), [euromaidanpress](https://euromaidanpress.com/2026/01/02/ukraine-economy-2026-growth-capped-2-labor-shortage/)).

## Key modeling parameters (condensed)
- FX: 44.4 now; budget-2026 avg 45.6; long-run drift ~8.6%/yr (episodic); managed-float drift ~7%/yr; inflation-differential-implied 3–7%/yr forward.
- Dividend repatriation: possible but capped (EUR 1 mn/mo, post-2023 profits) — relevant for foreign-routed structures; domestic reinvestment unrestricted.
- War-risk insurance cost: ~1% effective (state-subsidized, small caps) / 3.5–12% market rate; state direct-compensation cap UAH 10 mn.
- Tax wedge: 18% CIT, 23% on personal income, 10% on dividends, 23% on securities gains; Diia City 9%/5%; OVDP exempt.
- Labor: wage inflation 11–20%/yr nominal; booking floor pushes compliant payrolls to ≥UAH 21.6–25.9k/employee.
- Scenario weights: ceasefire ≤ end-2026 ~25–40% (market), EU accession ~2030–2034; GDP: war 1–2.5%, ceasefire 4–5%+.

Low-confidence items flagged inline: end-2021/end-2024 exact FX fixes, land-sale tax detail, UAH 30 mn vs 10 mn compensation cap discrepancy, exact live Polymarket odds, all post-2028 growth numbers.
