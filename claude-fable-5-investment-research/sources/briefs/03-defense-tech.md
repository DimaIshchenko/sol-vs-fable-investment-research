### The core structural constraint (thesis-critical)
- Defense-industrial **production capacity grew 35x since 2022: $1B → $35B** (mid-2025, Zelensky); projected **output $55B in 2026**; Kyiv Independent cites capacity of "$50B worth of weapons." **Domestic contracts funded only ~33% of capacity last year** — the excess-capacity gap that export liberalization (Section 4) is meant to monetize. [The Defense Post](https://thedefensepost.com/2026/07/03/ukraine-arms-exports/amp/), [Kyiv Independent](https://kyivindependent.com/from-garage-drones-to-nasdaq-has-ukraines-defense-tech-come-of-age/) **[HIGH]**
- Sector valuation: Ukrainian defense-tech industry valued at **$6.8B in 2026** (Kyiv School of Economics), ~6x its 2021 size; **~1,200 private defense companies** (2x+ vs 2023). [Kyiv Independent](https://kyivindependent.com/from-garage-drones-to-nasdaq-has-ukraines-defense-tech-come-of-age/) **[MED]**
- Partner financing of domestic production ("Danish model"/Zbroyari): **$1.5B raised in 2024** (vs $10B goal); >$500M via Danish model in year one; 9 countries + EU participating; MoD claims **>$6B secured for the defense industry in 2025** (headline; article 403'd — could not verify breakdown). [Euromaidan Press](https://euromaidanpress.com/2025/01/28/ukraines-defense-industry-draws-1-5-billion-in-foreign-investment-through-zbroyari-project/), [Wikipedia/ZBROYARI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ZBROYARI:_Manufacturing_Freedom), [globalsecurity.org](https://www.globalsecurity.org/wmd/library/news/ukraine/2026/01/ukraine-260103-ukraine-mod02.htm) **[MED — $6B unverified]**

[Ukrainska Pravda](https://www.pravda.com.ua/eng/articles/2026/05/01/8032647/), [Mezha](https://mezha.net/eng/bukvy/e624fb95_dot-chain_defence_accelerated/) **[HIGH]**

## 4. Export policy (revenue-upside thesis)

Timeline **[HIGH confidence overall]**:
- Since Feb 2022: de facto total export ban — all output legally reserved for Ukraine's Defense Forces.
- **Sept 2025**: Zelensky announces controlled export of *surplus* weapons + three export platforms (US, Europe, Middle East). [Wikipedia — Defense industry of Ukraine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Defense_industry_of_Ukraine)
- **Feb 2026**: NSDC announces first licenses planned for controlled surplus exports to approved partner states. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Defense_industry_of_Ukraine)
- **May 2026**: "Drone Deals" framework approved; officials call 2026 realistic for first contracts; Gulf buyers queuing for UAS/counter-UAS; also missiles, naval drones, battlefield-management software. [Defense News](https://www.defensenews.com/global/europe/2026/05/05/ukraine-could-lift-arms-exports-ban-this-year-as-would-be-buyers-line-up/)
- **July 1–3, 2026**: Cabinet approves "first transparent mechanism" for controlled exports — **27 partner nations (incl. 15 NATO members)**; minimum contract value $335K; 30-day application review; **levies to a special defense-industry development fund: 20% of proceeds on finished products/technologies, 30% on components**. [The Defense Post](https://thedefensepost.com/2026/07/03/ukraine-arms-exports/amp/), [UA.News](https://ua.news/en/ukraine/v-ukrayini-zapuskaiut-kontrolovanii-eksport-zbroyi-ta-oboronnikh-tekhnologii) **[HIGH]**
- Economic logic stated by MoD: domestic contracts cover ~1/3 of $35B capacity; foreign orders expected to generate "several times more revenue than domestic procurement." [The Defense Post](https://thedefensepost.com/2026/07/03/ukraine-arms-exports/amp/)
- Complementary channels already open: **Zbroyari: Manufacturing Freedom** (partners finance UA production: $1.5B in 2024), **Build with Ukraine** (licensed/JV production abroad — Skyeton in Slovakia/UK, MITS Industries in Denmark, General Cherry in US), **BraveTech EU** (€100M joint EU–Ukraine tech program, 50/50 EC/Kyiv; phase 2 scaling from June 2026; Airbus and Saab joined Brave1's network July 2026). [EU Commission](https://defence-industry-space.ec.europa.eu/eu-defence-industry/bravetech-eu_en), [The Defense Post](https://thedefensepost.com/2026/07/02/airbus-ukraine-brave1-partner/amp/) **[HIGH]**
- Counterweight: [Lawfare](https://www.lawfaremedia.org/article/the-red-tape-of-ukraine-s-semi-open-arms-exports) documents heavy case-by-case licensing red tape — "semi-open" regime, not free trade. **[MED]**

## 7. Key risks (with numbers)

1. **Margin cap on state procurement**: profit capped at **25% over documented costs** for unmanned systems/EW; disincentivizes cost reduction (profit falls in absolute UAH as costs fall); firms say they "cannot grow or invest in R&D." No plan to change it. [CSIS](https://www.csis.org/analysis/how-ukraine-rebuilt-its-military-acquisition-system-around-commercial-technology), [Breaking Defense](https://breakingdefense.com/2024/07/ukraines-startups-want-to-export-to-the-west-but-kyiv-standing-in-the-way/), [english.nv.ua](https://english.nv.ua/nation/ukrainian-drones-expert-reveals-what-hinders-uav-manufacturers-and-how-to-solve-this-problem-50521890.html) **[HIGH]**
2. **Export regime still "semi-open"**: case-by-case licensing, AFU-first supply condition, 20–30% export levies, $335K contract floor, 30-day reviews — bureaucratic drag on the upside thesis. [Lawfare](https://www.lawfaremedia.org/article/the-red-tape-of-ukraine-s-semi-open-arms-exports), [The Defense Post](https://thedefensepost.com/2026/07/03/ukraine-arms-exports/amp/) **[HIGH]**
3. **Customer concentration + governance/legal risk**: single dominant customer (MoD/DPA); Fire Point under NABU pricing probe; law-enforcement raid on a leading drone-company CEO fueled sector fears. [Kyiv Independent](https://kyivindependent.com/exclusive-maker-of-ukraines-prized-flamingo-cruise-missile-facing-corruption-probe/), [Kyiv Independent](https://kyivindependent.com/top-ukrainian-drone-ceo-searched-by-law-enforcement/) **[HIGH]**
4. **Post-war demand cliff vs EU rearmament hedge**: 2026 procurement (UAH 2.3T) is war-driven and EU-loan-financed (€60B of €90B facility); offset channels = EU SAFE/ReArm integration, BraveTech EU (€100M), Danish model, 10-year Gulf defense deals (Zelensky, March 2026). Workforce outflow and mil-to-civil conversion flagged as post-war risks. [Kyiv Independent](https://kyivindependent.com/from-garage-drones-to-nasdaq-has-ukraines-defense-tech-come-of-age/), [EU Commission](https://defence-industry-space.ec.europa.eu/eu-defence-industry/bravetech-eu_en) **[MED]**
5. **Data quality/froth**: 2025 VC totals vary 7x by methodology ($105M vs $129M vs $776M); Swarmer's +1,000% IPO on ~$0.3M revenue signals retail froth in "Ukraine defense" paper; TAF's $500M revenue is a third-party estimate; Fire Point valuations are founder/press claims. **[Flagged throughout]**
6. Physical risk (strikes on production), martial-law talent mobilization, and UAH FX (all UAH figures converted at ~41–42/USD) apply across the board.
