## 3. Diia City (special legal/tax regime, launched Feb 2022)

**Resident count growth:** ~600 (mid-2023) → ~800 (end-2023) → 1,396 (end-2024; +889 joined in 2024, residents doubled — [BRDO](https://brdo.com.ua/en/news/diia-city-in-2024-number-of-residents-doubles-defence-companies-emerge-as-revenue-leaders/)) → ~1,700 (early 2025, [Interfax](https://en.interfax.com.ua/news/general/1062011.html)) → 3,095 with 130k+ specialists (late 2025, [IT Research Ukraine 2025](https://scroll.media/en/2025/12/11/it-research-ukraine-2025/); [KMU](https://www.kmu.gov.ua/en/news/u-diiacity-vzhe-ponad-3-000-rezydentiv-i-130-000-it-fakhivtsiv)) → **4,000–4,300 residents, ~169k specialists (H1 2026)** ([GSER 2026](https://startupgenome.com/report/the-global-startup-ecosystem-report-2026/from-survival-to-scale-how-ukraine-became-one-of-the-world-s-most-unlikely-innovation-powerhouses)) [LC on the 4,300/169k pair — single source]. Growth driver: tax arbitrage vs closing FOP loopholes + mobilization-booking eligibility. Defense companies became revenue leaders among residents in 2024 (BRDO).

**Tax regime** ([PwC](https://www.pwc.com/ua/en/services/tax-legal-support-diia-city.html), [Kreston](https://kreston.ua/en/diia-city-for-startups-how-to-legally-save-on-taxes-and-grow-a-unicorn/), [Leinonen — 2025 changes](https://leinonen.eu/ukr/news/tax-and-social-security-changes-for-diia-city-residents-starting-in-2025/)):
- Corporate: choice of **18% CIT** or **9% exit-capital tax** (tax only on distributions; reinvested profit untaxed).
- Payroll (employees + gig contractors): **5% PIT** (vs 18% standard) + **5% military levy** (raised from 1.5% in Dec 2024) + unified social contribution at **22% of minimum wage only** (not of gross salary). Effective payroll burden ~10–12% vs ~41%+ standard employment.
- Dividends: PIT-exempt if distributed no more often than once every 2 years.
- **Qualification:** ≥9 employees/gig specialists; average salary ≥ €1,200/mo; ≥90% of income from qualified IT activities; checked monthly from the month after entry. Startup exemption (headcount/salary waived) for young companies below a revenue threshold.
- Residents paid UAH 4B taxes in 2022 (first year).

## 4. M&A market and valuation multiples

**Multiples — the core numbers:**
- **Current wartime bid for Ukrainian outsourcers: 3–4x EBITDA** for firms of ~100 specialists — explicitly stated as Euvic Ukraine's buy-box ([dev.ua](https://dev.ua/en/news/evict-1783399521), [InVenture](https://inventure.com.ua/en/news/ukraine/polish-it-group-euvic-raises-euro4-million-to-acquire-ukrainian-technology-companies)).
- **Per-engineer implied pricing:** Euvic Ukraine — ~500 specialists, business valued ~$10M ⇒ **~$20k/engineer** (2026). Pre-war benchmark: CoreValue/AVentures ~$40–60k per engineer (2018, [InVenture](https://inventure.com.ua/en/news/ukraine/aventures-has-invested-in-the-ukrainian-outsourcing-company-corevalue)). ⇒ **~50–65% discount vs pre-war per-engineer pricing** [LC — two data points, different companies].
- **Peer benchmark (global IT services, [Aventis Advisors](https://aventis-advisors.com/it-services-valuation-multiples/)):** median EV/EBITDA 10.2x (2015–2025); Q4 2025 8.8x; sub-$5M deals globally 5.9x EBITDA / 0.7x revenue; public software-development comps 9.8x (Mar 2026). ⇒ Ukrainian small outsourcers at 3–4x = **~35–45% discount vs same-size global peers (5.9x)** and ~55–65% vs the overall sector median. No published CEE-specific series found — gap.

**Deal flow ([KPMG M&A Radar 9m 2025](https://kpmg.com/ua/en/insights/2025/10/ma-radar-ukraine-9m-2025.html)):** all-Ukraine 9m 2025: 45 deals, $806M (+8% YoY), avg deal $34M; **inbound collapsed to $35M across 7 deals (vs $448M 9m 2024)** — foreign buyers remain scarce; the market is domestic consolidation + Ukrainian companies buying abroad ($329M outbound).

**Notable transactions 2023–26:**
- Qinshift (Norway/KKR-backed) acquired **Avenga** (large Ukrainian-rooted outsourcer), Feb 2024 ([PitchBook](https://pitchbook.com/profiles/company/117848-89), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avenga)) — value undisclosed.
- **Euvic Group (Poland)** roll-up: Artkai, Exoft (2023), 7Devs, Lampa Software, Diya; raised €4M from 3TS Capital (TCEE Fund IV) in mid-2026 to continue buying; aims to triple in 2 years ([dev.ua](https://dev.ua/en/news/evict-1783399521)).
- **IDAP Group** → Globaldev Group (2025), "market-average EBITDA multiple," staged buyout within a year, closed in 3 months ([Dnipro Investment Agency](https://dia.dp.gov.ua/en/ukrainian-web-developer-idap-group-becomes-part-of-an-international-it-company/)).
- Outbound: **Sigma Software → A Society Group (US), ~$10M** (Feb 2025); **Ciklum → GoSolve (Poland), ~$5M** (KPMG Radar).
- Domestic tech: **Kyivstar → Uklon, $155M** (2025; largest domestic deal).
- Buy-side interest from Israel IT in "several small Ukrainian outsourcers" (2023, [NV](https://english.nv.ua/business/israel-it-wants-to-buy-several-small-ukrainian-outsourcers-50319912.html)).
- ~50% of all Ukrainian M&A in 2023 was IT/telecom; boutique MergeWave closed 4 outsourcing deals in 2023 ([MergeWave](https://mergewave.capital/article/ma-market-2023-and-potential-trends-2024-ukraine-and-the-world/)).

## 6. Structural risks and upsides

**Risks**
1. **Mobilization exposure**: only 7% deferment coverage in <10-person teams; a 50–200-person target without "critically important" status can lose male staff with little notice. Mitigant: Diia City + critical-status booking (≤50% of liable staff).
2. **Talent pipeline erosion**: 18–22 exit rule (Aug 2025) + thin junior hiring; workforce seniorizing (avg 32.8y). 48% emigration intent [LC].
3. **AI cannibalization of commodity outsourcing**: front-end vacancies −515 over 6 months; iOS/Android at all-time lows (17–18 postings in June 2026); "classic web and mobile development are losing ground" ([dev.ua vacancy study](https://dev.ua/en/news/analityka-it-rynku-1783323369)). Ukrainian outsourcing is overweight exactly this commodity segment.
4. **Capital controls**: NBU still caps dividend repatriation at **€1M/month**, and only for dividends accrued from 1 Jan 2023; amounts above the cap allowed only within the "investment limit" = new FX capital brought in after 10 May 2025 ([NBU](https://bank.gov.ua/en/news/all/nbu-vprovadjuye-chergoviy-paket-pomyakshennya-valyutnih-obmejen), [Deloitte](https://www.deloitte.com/ua/en/services/tax/perspectives/tax-and-legal-alert-2025-08-07.html)). For a $2–5M buyer this is workable (annual profit < €1M/mo cap) but must be modeled.
5. Foreign inbound M&A still depressed ($35M, 9m 2025) — thin exit market; assume long hold or sale to Polish/domestic consolidators.

**Upsides**
1. **Entry pricing**: 3–4x EBITDA / ~$20k per engineer vs 5.9–10x peers — multiple re-rating on ceasefire/EU accession is the core equity story.
2. **Return migration**: abroad-count already fell 62–64k → 58k (2025); 27% of companies opened new offices in 2025.
3. **Defense-tech crossover**: fastest-growing vertical (20x funding growth in 2 years, record AI/ML/hardware/cyber vacancies, Brave1 pipeline, export legalization plans) — outsourcing firms can redeploy engineering capacity; defense residents already lead Diia City revenues.
4. Diia City tax regime is a durable ~30pp payroll-cost advantage vs EU peers; sector CEO sentiment turned positive for 2026 (33% expect industry growth vs 15.6% a year earlier).
5. Reconstruction ("RebuilTech") optionality; global IT spending +10.8% in 2026 (Gartner via dev.ua).

## 7. Feasibility for a $2–5M investor

**Arithmetic:** a 100-person Ukrainian outsourcer ≈ $5–7M revenue (blended ~$50–70k revenue/engineer), 10–20% EBITDA margin ⇒ $0.6–1.4M EBITDA ⇒ **$2–5M at the prevailing 3–4x — exactly this budget**. Per-engineer check: 100–200 engineers × ~$20k = $2–4M. Confirmed live demand at this size: Euvic (100-specialist targets, 3–4x), Israel IT (several small outsourcers), Globaldev/IDAP precedent (3-month close, staged earn-out with full buyout in 12 months — the standard wartime structure).

**Where deals are brokered:**
- **MergeWave Capital** ([mergewave.capital](https://mergewave.capital/)) — the dominant sell-side boutique for lower-mid-market IT outsourcing/B2B SaaS; 50+ deals in 5 years; exclusive advisor to Euvic's roll-up. First call for proprietary deal flow.
- **InVenture Investment Group** ([inventure.com.ua](https://inventure.com.ua/en/investments/business)) — M&A marketplace + IB; runs Ukraine's deal database; lists IT businesses for sale.
- Capital Times, KPMG/Big4 Ukraine corporate finance — for larger or auctioned assets.
- Minority-stake route: co-investing alongside **Horizon Capital** (Fund IV, $350M; but their checks are $10–30M for $50–150M companies — above this budget), or startup tickets via USF/angel syndicates; realistic $2–5M minority plays are direct secondaries in Diia City product companies, not intermediated.

**Deal-structure notes:** buy the offshore TopCo (most outsourcers bill via US/EE entities — also sidesteps NBU repatriation friction); earn-outs of 12–24 months are standard; verify Diia City residency + critical-enterprise booking quota transfer; check client concentration and share of staff who are draft-liable males without deferment.

## Gaps / caveats
- No official NBU per-year table was fetched directly from bank.gov.ua (figures are NBU data via IT Ukraine Assoc., Lviv IT Cluster, Interfax, dev.ua — consistent across sources).
- No published EV/EBITDA series specific to Ukrainian or CEE IT services deals exists; 3–4x is buyer-stated (Euvic), not a closed-deal median. Pre-war Ukrainian deal multiples (2021) were not publicly disclosed — the pre-war comparison rests on the $40–60k/engineer CoreValue datapoint.
- Employment counts vary ±40k by methodology (DOU-registered vs tax-registered vs survey).
- 2025 defense-tech total ($105M vs $129M) and 2025 VC total ($498M vs $526M) conflict between AIN/AVentures/Brave1 — use ranges.
- Diia City jump 3,095 → 4,000–4,300 within ~6 months is plausible (booking incentive) but the 4,300/169k figure is single-source (Startup Genome).
