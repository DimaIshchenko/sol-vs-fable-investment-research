### Offtake regimes
- **Green tariff (feed-in): closed to new commercial/utility projects.** Legacy projects keep it to 31 Dec 2029/"until 2030". New eligibility only: households ≤30 kW — **UAH 6.13/kWh** (~$0.14); small business/energy co-ops ≤150 kW roof/façade — **UAH 5.49/kWh** (rates set July 2026; quarterly FX-recalc; a June source cites 6.0386 UAH/kWh — minor discrepancy 🟡). 🟢 [pv-magazine, 9 Jul 2026](https://www.pv-magazine.com/2026/07/09/ukraine-updates-solar-feed-in-tariffs/); [ProfEnergy](https://profenergy.com.ua/en/blog/zelenyy-taryf-2026/)
- **CfD/feed-in auctions** exist in law (Law 2712-VIII, quotas approved for 2025 — [Dentons](https://www.dentons.com/en/insights/articles/2024/december/20/ukrainian-government-approves-quotas-and-res-auction-schedule-for-2025)) but **have not run at scale**. 🟡 [VoxUkraine White Book 2025](https://voxukraine.org/en/white-book-of-reforms-2025-energy-sector-reforms-in-ukraine)
- **Corporate PPAs**: legal, 10–20 yr terms, growing but slow — "shortage of creditworthy buyers." 🟡 [Legal500](https://www.legal500.com/developments/thought-leadership/corporate-ppas-a-modern-alternative-to-green-tariffs/) **GAP: no publicly disclosed signed corporate PPA with price found.** New projects are effectively merchant (DAM via trader) or PPA-bespoke.

## 7. GRID DESTRUCTION CONTEXT (demand driver for DG/BESS)

- Cumulative war losses: **43.5 GW of generation lost; ~17.5 GW operational in 2025; ~11.5 GW available Feb 2026** 🟡 [Kyiv Post](https://www.kyivpost.com/post/64454), [Ukrainian Review](https://theukrainianreview.info/power-outages-as-the-new-reality-the-state-and-prospects-of-ukraines-energy-system/) — medium confidence, definitions vary (installed vs available).
- 🟢 [Green Deal Ukraina (Helmholtz/DiXi), 26 Nov 2025](https://greendealukraina.org/assets/images/reports/electricity-and-gas-supply-in-ukraine-winter-2025-26.pdf) — read in full:
  - Nov 2025 attacks dropped **available conventional capacity below 13 GW** (first time since Apr 2024); of ~48 GW installed (ex-wind/PV), roughly half destroyed/occupied, further tranche damaged.
  - RES capacity fell **9.6 GW (Jan 2022) → 7.4 GW (Oct 2025)** via occupation/destruction.
  - Attack phases: 2022/23 HV substations → 2023/24–24/25 thermal+hydro → **2025/26 gas production, CHPs, DSO substations**. Oct 2025 strikes hit **~60% of gas production** (52 → ~21 mcm/d; ~95% reportedly restored by mid-Nov, unconfirmed).
  - **Winter 2025/26 modeled load shedding (baseline): 35% of hours, ~2 TWh unserved, max 5.5 GW = 34% of peak load;** heat-shock scenario: 3 TWh, max 6.5 GW, 50% of hours. Demand Dec-25–Mar-26: 32 TWh net.
  - Scheduled household outages jumped to **9–12+ h/day** in most eastern/central oblasts by mid-Nov 2025.
- Winter 25/26 actual: Jan 2026 energy emergency at −20°C, millions without power; typical deficit 1–4 GW depending on strikes. 🟡 [UN HRMMU](https://ukraine.ohchr.org/en/Attacks-against-Ukraine-s-energy-infrastructure-and-update-on-the-human-rights-situation-in-Ukraine-1-December-2025-31-May-2026); [NV expert](https://english.nv.ua/nation/winter-2025-2026-expert-assesses-power-cuts-blackouts-consequences-of-attacks-outage-durating-50563941.html)
- Structural takeaway for the model: **the binding constraint is flexibility (evening peak), not energy** — nuclear base survives; thermal/hydro flex destroyed. This is precisely the BESS/gas-piston/peak-solar value pool, and it explains the EUR-fixed 5-yr Ukrenergo capacity contracts.
